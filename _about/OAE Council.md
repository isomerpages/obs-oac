and</p></li><li><p>Continue to&nbsp;<strong>engage with the OAE community</strong>&nbsp;and propose schemes, resources, pathways and other enabling structures to support growth and transformation of the sector.</p></li></ol><h3>Long Term Development of the OAE Sector</h3><div class="isomer-image-wrapper"><img style="width: 100%" height="auto" width="100%" alt="" src="/images/long%20term.png"></div><p>In addition to promoting the adoption of new national standards, the OAE Council will monitor incident rates and adoption rate of national standards, active participation numbers, currency of standards, number of certified OAE providers and non-compliance*. Further measures (regulatory or statutory) if necessary, will be recommended in updates to MCCY.</p><p>*E.g., incident reporting, periodic safety reviews, regular certification assessments, etc.</p><h3>Vision for our OAE Sector</h3><p>The OAE Council has identified two long-term outcomes for the OAE Sector:</p><ul data-tight="true" class="tight"><li><p>A <strong>self-regulated</strong> sector that is <strong>highly responsible</strong> in ensuring safe practices while being <strong>accountable</strong> to themselves, the sector and the consumers; and</p></li><li><p>A <strong>thriving sector</strong> that strives to be <strong>exemplary</strong> in professionalism, continuous development, and designing and delivering quality OAE programmes.</p></li></ul><p>This vision can be achieved through the commitment of all OAE stakeholders and OAE Council in areas of trust-building, knowledge-sharing and collective responsibility of uplifting the sector.</p><div class="isomer-image-wrapper"><img style="width: 100%" height="auto" width="100%" alt="" src="/images/outcomes.png"></div><p></p>
